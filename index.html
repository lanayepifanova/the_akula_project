Our Mission

The Akula Project is dedicated to supporting first-generation, low-income, and situationally disadvantaged students by providing free tutoring, bootcamps, and mentorship programs.

Founded by Lana Yepifanova, inspired by the work of Vikram Akula, a pioneer in microfinance. Fun fact: "Akula" means "shark" in Russian — symbolizing resilience, adaptability, and strength.

Free Math Tutoring

We offer tutoring in Algebra, Pre-Calculus, Calculus, and more. One-on-one virtual or in-person sessions designed to fit your schedule.

Hello, my name is Lana Yepifanova and I am a rising junior at Rice University studying Computational and Applied Mathematics, with a minor in Statistics. I attended Townsend Harris High School and graduated with a 4.0 GPA and a 1550 SAT. I am very excited to be your tutor this summer!

The condition for participating in this tutoring program is that you must self-identify as low-income and/or first-generation and/or economically or situationally disadvantaged in some way. This includes students struggling with mental health, learning disabilities, or past academic challenges. I will not ask for any proof — this program operates on an Honor System. I am happy to teach and support you/your student for free.

Availability

  • Times: Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays from 9am–9pm
  • Dates: June 6th to August 10th
  • Location: Forest Hills (Queens), NY. I can travel within 30 minutes by subway/bus/bike. Otherwise, we can meet halfway or in Forest Hills.

I can bring prepared materials, content, and practice questions, or work with your current packets or SAT tests. Sessions are flexible, with up to 0–3 meetings per week, depending on mutual availability. I will coordinate consistent scheduling where possible but remain flexible to your summer plans!

Topics Covered

  • Middle School: Linear Equations, Systems of Equations, Basic Probability & Statistics, Algebra I
  • High School: Geometry, Algebra II/Trigonometry, Pre-Calculus, Calculus AB/BC, SAT Math
  • Early College: Multivariable Calculus, Ordinary Differential Equations, Linear Algebra

NYC High School Bootcamps

Explore careers in Computer Science and Finance through our free summer bootcamps designed for motivated high school students.

Thank you for your interest in the NYC High School bootcamps! My goal with this program is to give you exposure, so that you can be more prepared and feel less anxious when you start college. Recruiting cycles for these careers tend to creep up early in freshman and sophomore year, so especially if you are First-Gen and/or Low-Income, I encourage you to apply.

Tracks Offered

Computer Science Track

This track focuses on developing your CS resume, learning about careers (software engineering, quantitative trading, data science, etc), setting up your GitHub, and practicing "Easy" LeetCode problems. Sessions include small lectures but are mostly project-based, focusing on interview-relevant skills.

Finance Track

This track focuses on developing your finance resume, exploring careers (investment banking, asset management, private equity, venture capital, equity research, sales & trading, etc), and practicing fundamental concepts through group activities and mock interviews. Sessions include a small lecture but are largely interactive.
